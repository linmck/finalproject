The Data

We are interested in exploring the Sustainable Development Goals set forth by The World Bank that they would like to achieve by 2030. These goals are measured by several World Development Indicators (WDI). We will select several indicators to help predict the development of a country as measured by the Human Development Index. The Human Development Index (HDI) is a comprehensive measure of the development of a country that takes into account factors other than just GDP such as health, education, infrastructure, etc.

The Machine Learning Model

For this project, we used a multilinear regression model since HDI was numerical and we had a few features/indicators to consider. During the charting and modelling process we discovered that some indicators, such as life expectancy and agriculture, weighed on the model much more heavily than others because they had strong linear relationships individually with HDI. Surprisingly GDP and GNI did not have much affect on HDI. The ML model helped us to identify the strongest predictors of HDI.

Conclusion

Economists often use GDP as a measure of a country's "success" or development. However, our research has shown that other indicators, such as education or gender equality, affect HDI much more than GDP. We hope that leaders and economists can explore other indicators besides just GDP and GNI to improve human development within their countries and the global community.
